### 1. Brown et al. 2018 — Alpha-Globin Domain

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Title** | A tissue-specific self-interacting chromatin domain forms independently of enhancer-promoter interactions |
| **Authors** | Jill M. Brown, Nigel A. Roberts, ..., Douglas R. Higgs, Veronica J. Buckle |
| **Journal** | Nature Communications |
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **DOI** | 10.1038/s41467-018-06248-4 |
| **PDF status** | ✅ Full text extracted (74,722 chars) |

**Key verified claims (direct quotes):**

> "In engineered mice with a homozygous double knockout of the two major α-globin enhancers (DKO for MCS-R1 and MCS-R2), nascent transcription from the α-globin locus is **reduced by 90%**. NG Capture-C analysis indicates that the **self-interacting domain still forms** in erythroblasts in the absence of the major enhancers."

> "Formation of this domain does not rely on interactions between the α-globin genes and their major enhancers, suggesting a **transcription-independent mechanism** for establishment of the domain."

> "However, absence of the major enhancers **does alter internal domain interactions**."

> "We show that this region forms an erythroid-specific, decompacted, self-interacting domain, delimited by frequently apposed **CTCF/cohesin binding sites** early in terminal erythroid differentiation."

**What ARCHCODE can use:**

| Aspect | Application |
|---|---|
| **Limitation** | LSSIM measures topology disruption, not expression consequence. Brown shows domain topology persists even when transcription drops 90%. → Cite in Limitations section |
| **Mechanism** | CTCF/cohesin delimit domain boundaries (tissue-specific). Supports our engine design using CTCF sites as barriers |
| **Relevance to Class B** | Domain intact ≠ regulatory function intact → This explains why Class B variants (architecture-driven) may disrupt *internal* interactions while domain boundary remains visible |
| **Specific to erythroid** | Tissue-specific domain formation in alpha-globin (human HBA1 analog) directly relevant to HBB/erythroid context |

**Action item:** Add to Limitations in `body_content.typ`:
> "Consistently, Brown et al. showed that the alpha-globin self-interacting domain persists even after deletion of major enhancers that reduce transcription by 90%, indicating that structural topology and expression consequence can dissociate (Brown et al., Nat Commun 2018)."

### 2. Zuin et al. 2022 — Nonlinear Enhancer-Promoter Control

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Nonlinear control of transcription through enhancer–promoter interactions |
| **Authors** | Jessica Zuin, Gregory Roth, Yinxiu Zhan, ..., Luca Giorgetti |
| **Journal** | Nature |
| **Year** | 2022 |
| **DOI** | 10.1038/s41586-022-04570-y |
| **PDF status** | ✅ Full text extracted (150,955 chars) |

**Key verified claims (direct quotes):**

> "A quantitative analysis of hundreds of cell lines reveals that the transcriptional effect of an enhancer depends on its contact probabilities with the promoter through a **nonlinear relationship**."

> "Contact probabilities steeply decayed with increasing genomic distance from the promoter, fell considerably while approaching TAD boundaries (from 1 to around **0.05**) and further dropped by a **factor of around 3 across boundaries**."

> "enhancer strength also determines absolute transcription levels as well as the **sensitivity of a promoter to CTCF-mediated transcriptional insulation**."

> "the enhancer would be no longer able to activate the promoter outside the approximately **threefold drop** in contact probabilities generated by TAD boundaries"

> "Mathematical modelling suggests that nonlinearity might arise from transient enhancer–promoter interactions being translated into **slower promoter bursting dynamics** in individual cells."

**What ARCHCODE can use:**

| Aspect | Application |
|---|---|
| **Nonlinear ΔLSSIM → function** | Small structural disruption near the sigmoid threshold can have large transcriptional effects. Large ΔLSSIM in low-contact regions may have smaller impact. Supports why we use a **threshold** (LSSIM < 0.95) rather than continuous score |
| **~3× contact drop at TAD boundaries** | Quantitative benchmark. If a variant shifts contact probability by 3×, it effectively crosses the functional threshold. ΔLSSIM may proxy this |
| **CTCF insulation sensitivity** | Enhancer strength modulates sensitivity to CTCF-mediated insulation — supports our Class E (tissue-mismatch) interpretation: weak tissue enhancers are more sensitive to insulation |
| **Discussion framing** | Nonlinearity means our LSSIM structural score does not map linearly to functional effect — honest and supported by data |

**Action item:** Add to Discussion in `body_content.typ`:
> "The nonlinear relationship between contact probability and transcription (Zuin et al., Nature 2022) provides mechanistic grounding for our threshold-based classification: variants that reduce contact probability beyond a critical value may disproportionately affect transcriptional output, consistent with the sigmoidal burst-frequency dependence on enhancer–promoter contact probability."
