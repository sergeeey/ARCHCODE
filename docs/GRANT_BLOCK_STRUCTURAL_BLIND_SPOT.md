# Grant Block: Structural Blind Spot — Experimental Validation

**Title (suggested):** *Experimental validation of ARCHCODE structural blind spot: RT-PCR and Capture Hi-C for pearl variants.*

**Summary (for grant application):**  
ARCHCODE, a physics-based 3D chromatin loop extrusion model, identifies 27 HBB "pearl" variants that are ClinVar pathogenic and structurally disruptive in silico (LSSIM &lt; 0.95) but invisible to VEP, SpliceAI, CADD, and MPRA — demonstrating a structural blind spot of sequence-based and episomal predictors. Nine orthogonal computational lines support this finding; in silico Virtual CRISPR predicts ~17% promoter–LCR contact drop for top promoter pearls. This work package funds **experimental validation** in erythroid cell models (K562 and/or HUDEP-2): (1) RT-PCR to quantify aberrant splicing or transcript change at pearl positions; (2) Capture Hi-C at the HBB locus to measure promoter–LCR contact in wild-type vs variant. Variant panel and success criteria are defined in a formal cross-lab protocol (see `docs/VALIDATION_CROSSLAB_PROTOCOL.md` and shortlist `results/pearl_validation_shortlist.json`). Outcomes (positive, negative, or inconclusive) will be reported with full provenance; no clinical reclassification without validation. Collaboration with a lab that has erythroid lines and Hi-C/RT-PCR capacity is anticipated.

**Key deliverables:**  
- Validated protocol and variant panel; RT-PCR and/or Capture Hi-C data for ≥3 priority pearl variants; manuscript update or companion preprint with experimental results.
